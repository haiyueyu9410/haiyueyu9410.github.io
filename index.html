About

I am a Senior Applied Scientist at Snowflake, where I build end-to-end ML systems and design LLM-powered agents for enterprise applications. My current work includes developing agentic workflows, LLM-as-a-Judge evaluation pipelines, and Agent GPA (Goal–Plan–Action) frameworks to improve agent reliability and alignment.

I hold a PhD in Economics from Princeton University with a Graduate Certificate in Statistics and Machine Learning. My research applies machine learning to complex prediction problems, with work forthcoming in the Journal of Finance.

Previously, I was a quantitative researcher at Two Sigma, where I built predictive models and led causal inference research for systematic macro strategies. I am passionate about advancing AI capabilities and building AI systems that are reliable, interpretable, and aligned with human intent.

Research Interests
